In this experiment you will be randomly paired with another person for a sequence of rounds.

Each sequence of rounds is referred to as a match.

The length of a match is randomly determined. After each round, there is a {{continuation_chance}}% probability that the match will continue for at least another round. So, for instance, if you are in round 2, the probability there will be a third round is {{continuation_chance}}% and if you are in round 9, the probability there will be a tenth round is also {{continuation_chance}}%.

This is how the length of a match is determined: at the end of each round, the computer rolls a fair 100-sided die. If the die comes up {{die_threshold_plus_one}} or more we do not continue to another round, and the match is over. If the die comes up {{continuation_chance}} or less, we continue to another round of the same match. We continue to more rounds, until {{die_threshold_plus_one}} or more is rolled at the end of a round and the match ends.

Once a match ends, you will be randomly paired with another person for a new match.

In each round, you and the person you are paired with choose simultaneously between two actions, Action 1 and Action 2.

This table shows the payoff associated with each choice:
The Other Participant
Action 1 Action 2
You Action 1 {{ C.BOTH_COOPERATE_PAYOFF }}, {{ C.BOTH_COOPERATE_PAYOFF }} 0, {{ C.BETRAY_PAYOFF }}
Action 2 {{ C.BETRAY_PAYOFF }}, 0 {{ C.BOTH_DEFECT_PAYOFF }}, {{ C.BOTH_DEFECT_PAYOFF }}

The first entry in each cell represents your payoff, while the second entry represents the payoff of the person you are matched with.

  • If you select Action 1 and the other selects Action 1, you each make 32.
  • If you select Action 1 and the other selects Action 2, you make 12 while the other makes 50.
  • If you select Action 2 and the other selects Action 1, you make 50 while the other makes 12.
  • If you select Action 2 and the other selects Action 2, you each make 25.

Once you and the person you are paired with have made your choices, those choices will be highlighted and your payoff for the round will appear.

The experiment is over at the first match to end after 1 hour of play.

You will earn €{{C.CONVERSION_RATE}} for every point scored, in addition to the €5 show-up fee.
